Powerbank calculator

This builder helps you choose your van & offgrid charging source, battery, and accessories by showing you clearly how much power goes into and out of your battery based on charging method and chosen accessories.

1) Choosing a charging source: Your charging source will most likely be solar if you're going for offgrid. And maybe aided by a generator. If you have a van, you also have the option for DC-to-DC chargers. Which provide a lot of power regardless of how sunny it is. They're a great option especially if you have a lithium battery. If you plan to drive often, you may not need a solar panel at all. Select the charging source you want in the form below.

2) Choosing a battery: It's possible to run a lot of accessories directly from the solar panel alone, without a battery. But this way you only have power when the sun is out.
The capacity of your battery will depend on how long you need power without the sun, and if you want to run accessories that need more power than the solar panel can provide on its own.

3) Choosing accessories: the most common things people want are a way to keep food cold; A way to illuminate at night; And some sort of heating or cooling.
You can search popular options and add them to your accessories list by clicking on them. The tool will automatically calculate how long your selected battery will last based on the accessories you choose.
Some accessories don't need to be on all the time, such as heaters. After adding an accessory you can choose how long you choose to use it per day, and this will greatly increase your battery time.

1) Charge time is affected first and foremost by the type of battery.

2) Lithium batteries can "pull" much more charge and much faster than lead-acid or AGM batteries. For example, if you hook up a lead-acid battery to a 600 watt charging source, the lead-acid battery would absorb about 10-20% of that power. Meanwhile, a lithium battery hooked to the same power source would absorb 100% of that power.

3) When charging from a solar panel you have to take into account that the sun isn't always at an optimal angle. This heavily affects how effective a solar panel is.

4) Lithium batteries cannot charge below 0 degrees celcius
