Life

If you're interested in knowing how I went from robotics to business consulting, take a look at some life events that made it happen.

1992

Born

Born in a very rural and isolated part of Portugal

1997

First contacts with Electronics

After some electronic toys broke, my mom taught me how to open them up and fix simple things such as loose wires.

2001

First robot cybot

A weekly-issue robotics kit came out in the market, I only bought the first 16 weeks due to lack of money, but this assured basic functionality. It was my first time controlling a machine via sensors, and a good introduction to robotics.

Between this and 2007 I deepened my electronics theory knowledge via magazines and books, I had no internet access at this point.

2002

Ilness and nearly died

Due to complications in getting adequate treatment, a simple appendicitis turned into an acute appendicitis with peritoneal abscess. According to the surgeon I was half an hour away from death. This really got in the way of projects.

2005

Web development with Frontpage

My first contact ever with HTML&CSS was with Microsoft frontpage when it was still around, and I made my first web pages before having internet.

2008

microcontrollers and internet

Getting internet allowed me to do better and more up-to-date searches, which lead to buying an Arduino board and loading my first programs onto it. I didn't touch frontpage for a while as I was focusing 100% on hardware and robotics.

2009-2011

Lots of Robots!

One project lead to another, and I ended up with many robots and general projects on my desk, from simple AM transmitters to more complex robots with navigation algorithms.

2010

First site for Robotics

I continued my robotics projects, and after a while I wanted a site where I could showcase them. I figured if I could make robots, a site wouldn't be hard. This lead to going into HTML/CSS again, but this time adding JavaScript, PHP, and MySQL to the mix. I was already doing basic freelance work at this point.

2013

Became volunteer Firefighter

I continued my robotics projects, and after a while I wanted a site where I could showcase them. I figured if I could make robots, a site wouldn't be hard. This lead to going into HTML/CSS again, but this time adding JavaScript, PHP, and MySQL to the mix. I was already doing basic freelance work at this point.
